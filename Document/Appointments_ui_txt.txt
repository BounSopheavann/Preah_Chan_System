APPOINTMENTS MODULE UI SPECIFICATION
Dental Clinic Management System

====================================================
1. PAGE OVERVIEW
====================================================

Purpose
The Appointments page is the operational scheduling hub for the dental clinic. It is used to create, review, manage, and reschedule appointments for patients, dentists, and clinic staff.

Who uses it
- Receptionists use it to book new visits, confirm attendance, and manage daily schedules.
- Dentists use it to review their assigned appointments and prepare for treatment sessions.
- Assistants use it to support patient check-in, room preparation, and visit coordination.
- Administrators use it to monitor scheduling capacity, resolve conflicts, and review clinic utilization.

Why it exists
- To keep appointment booking centralized and organized.
- To reduce double-booking, missed visits, and manual scheduling errors.
- To connect patient records, doctors, treatment plans, and billing actions in one workflow.

How it fits into clinic workflow
- Appointments are created from patient records, front desk requests, or direct scheduling actions.
- Each appointment links to a patient, provider, service type, status, and visit notes.
- Follow-up treatment plans, recalls, and invoice workflows can all be linked back to scheduled visits.
- The page acts as the main coordination layer between patient registration and care delivery.

====================================================
2. PAGE LAYOUT
====================================================

Overall structure
The page uses a full-width dashboard layout with a fixed application shell, page header, scheduling controls, a calendar or appointment list view, and contextual side panels or modals.

Header
- Located at the top of the page content area.
- Contains the page title, number of appointments, and primary actions.
- Keeps key actions visible without forcing users to search through the schedule.

Breadcrumb
- Sits below or near the page title.
- Shows navigation context such as Dashboard > Appointments.
- Helps users understand where they are within the clinic system.

Search bar
- Positioned in the header area or above the appointment list.
- Supports quick lookup by patient name, appointment ID, or provider.

Filter bar
- Located next to or below search.
- Provides filtering by status, date, dentist, appointment type, and room or clinic branch.

Action buttons
- Positioned to the right of the header or in a compact group.
- Includes New Appointment, Today, Day/Week/Month View, and Export or Print actions.

Appointment view
- Main content block.
- Can be displayed as a calendar, daily schedule, or searchable list depending on the selected mode.

Pagination or timeline controls
- Controls schedule navigation across date ranges.
- Helps users move between future and past booked visits.

Side panels
- Optional slide-in panel for quick patient preview, booking details, or appointment notes.
- Should not block the whole page unless a destructive action is being confirmed.

Empty state
- Shown when there are no appointments in the selected range.
- Encourages users to create a new booking or change the selected date filter.

Loading state
- Shown during schedule fetches, rescheduling, or refresh operations.
- Uses skeleton placeholders or shimmer effects.

====================================================
3. HEADER
====================================================

Page title
- Label: Appointments.
- Indicates the current module and the main function of the page.

Total appointment count
- Shows the number of visible appointments or the total count in the selected date range.
- Updates when filters or calendar views are applied.
- Helps staff understand demand and daily load.

New Appointment button
- Primary button.
- Opens the appointment creation form.
- Used by receptionists, assistants, and administrators to book visits.

Today button
- Quickly redirects the scheduler to the current day.
- Helps staff work with live daily operations.

View toggle
- Supports Day, Week, Month, or List view.
- Makes scheduling easier depending on the user's task.

Search
- Quick access search is often placed in the header for immediate lookup.
- Accepts patient name, appointment ID, provider, or visit reason.

====================================================
4. SEARCH & FILTERS
====================================================

Search by patient name
- Searches patient first name, last name, or full name.
- Supports partial matching and case-insensitive input.
- Results should update as the user types or after pressing Enter.

Appointment ID
- Finds a booking by its unique appointment code or system ID.
- Useful for troubleshooting and fast retrieval.

Dentist / Provider
- Filters appointments by assigned clinician or provider.
- Important for schedule ownership and staff coordination.

Status
- Filters by status such as Scheduled, Confirmed, Checked In, In Progress, Completed, Cancelled, or No Show.
- Helps staff focus on urgent or incomplete visits.

Appointment type
- Filters by service category such as Consultation, Cleaning, Checkup, Extraction, Whitening, or Follow-up.
- Useful for resource planning and clinic analytics.

Date range
- Filters appointments by day, week, month, or custom date period.
- Supports staff planning and historical review.

Patient contact or Telegram
- Can be used to find a booking through a linked communication channel.
- Helps front desk staff identify patients quickly.

Filter behavior
- Filters can be combined.
- Search and filter results should update the schedule without losing the user's current view context.
- Active filters should be visible as chips or tags that can be cleared individually.
- A Reset Filters action should restore the default calendar/list state.

====================================================
5. APPOINTMENT TABLE / SCHEDULE VIEW
====================================================

Schedule behavior
- The schedule is the main browsing surface for the Appointments module.
- Calendar or list view should support drag-and-drop rescheduling where appropriate.
- Rows or blocks should be clickable for quick details.

Appointment ID
- Purpose: Unique booking identifier.
- Example value: AP-000842

Patient Name
- Purpose: Main identity of the appointment.
- Example value: Sok Phearith

Provider
- Purpose: Assigned dentist or clinician.
- Example value: Dr. Lina Sok

Type
- Purpose: Service requested or appointment category.
- Example value: Consultation

Date & Time
- Purpose: Start time and scheduled duration.
- Example value: 18 Jul 2026, 10:30 AM

Status
- Purpose: Indicates whether the appointment is booked, confirmed, in progress, or completed.
- Example value: Confirmed

Room / Chair
- Purpose: Assigned treatment location.
- Example value: Room 2

Contact
- Purpose: Patient phone or Telegram for confirmation and reminders.
- Example value: +855 12 345 678

Actions
- Purpose: Offers row-level or block-level actions.
- Example value: View, Edit, Check In, Cancel, Reschedule

Recommended row presentation
- Patient name should be visually stronger than supporting metadata.
- Status should use clear badges for fast recognition.
- Time slots and providers should be easy to scan at a glance.

Waiting Queue section
- A dedicated queue card should be displayed below the main Appointments table.
- This section is intended for patients who have already completed check-in and are waiting for treatment.
- Only appointments with status Checked-in should appear in this queue.
- Booked, Confirmed, Completed, Cancelled, and No-show statuses must not be displayed here.
- The queue must automatically update whenever a patient is checked in or moved to In Chair.

Waiting Queue table columns
- Queue No.
- Patient
- Patient Code
- Dentist
- Appointment Time
- Check-in Time
- Waiting Time
- Priority
- Medical Alert
- Status
- Actions

Waiting Queue behavior
- Waiting time is calculated as Current Time - Check-in Time.
- The displayed time should refresh automatically every minute.
- Priority badges are shown as Normal, High, or Emergency.
- Emergency should always appear at the top of the queue.
- Sorting order should be Emergency first, then Appointment Time, then Check-in Time.
- Medical alerts such as Allergy, Medical Condition, or Special Alert should display a warning icon and tooltip.
- The queue action set should include View, Edit Patient, and Call Patient.
- When Call Patient is selected, a confirmation dialog should ask: "Send this patient to the treatment chair?"
- Confirming the action updates the appointment from Checked-in to In Chair and removes it immediately from the Waiting Queue.

Waiting Queue empty state
- If no patients are waiting, display a friendly empty illustration with the message: "No patients are currently waiting."

Responsive behavior
- Desktop: full table layout.
- Tablet: compact table with reduced column width.
- Mobile: card-based layout for queue items.

====================================================
6. ACTION BUTTONS
====================================================

View
- Opens the appointment detail panel or full schedule item view.
- Should be available to all roles with read access.
- Navigation should preserve the current date range or schedule context where possible.

Edit
- Opens the appointment edit form.
- Available only to users with editing permission.
- Should preload current appointment details.

Check In
- Marks the appointment as Checked In.
- Typically used by reception or front desk staff once the patient arrives.

Reschedule
- Changes the appointment date or time.
- Should be supported with a simple and safe confirmation flow.

Cancel
- Marks the appointment as cancelled.
- Should require confirmation if the action is destructive or affects provider availability.

Add Note
- Opens a quick note or visit comment area.
- Useful for front desk staff, assistants, and clinicians.

Patient Profile
- Opens the linked patient profile for a quick overview of medical or billing context.
- Useful when staff need to confirm patient history before the visit.

Billing
- Opens the invoice or payment area associated with the appointment if applicable.
- Used by front desk and finance roles.

Delete
- Opens a confirmation modal before removal.
- Restricted to users with deletion permission.
- Should require explicit confirmation because it affects scheduling history.

====================================================
7. APPOINTMENT DETAIL VIEW
====================================================

Page purpose
The Appointment Detail view is the central record for one scheduled visit. It shows the patient, provider, service, timing, status, notes, and linked actions.

Layout
- Top summary section with patient identity and appointment status.
- Contextual action buttons for edit, check in, reschedule, and cancel.
- Related information sections for patient profile, notes, and billing.

Overview section
- Shows appointment date, time, duration, patient, provider, and service type.
- Designed for fast first review by front desk and clinical staff.

Patient section
- Displays patient name, phone, Telegram, and any medical or account context relevant to the visit.
- Useful for quick confirmation before treatment.

Clinical / Visit Notes
- Contains short notes added by reception, assistants, or clinicians.
- Should be time-stamped and attributed to the author.

Service Information
- Lists the planned dental procedure, appointment reason, and any treatment plan reference.
- Helps staff understand what is happening during the visit.

History / Linked Records
- Shows related records such as patient history, invoices, or previous appointments.
- Useful for continuity of care.

Status Log
- Displays a timeline of changes such as created, confirmed, checked in, rescheduled, or cancelled.
- Useful for accountability and operational visibility.

====================================================
8. NEW APPOINTMENT FORM
====================================================

Form purpose
The New Appointment form creates a new booking with the minimum information needed for scheduling, patient communication, and service planning.

Patient
- Required.
- Select patient from existing records or create a quick patient lookup.
- Should display the selected patient name and contact details.

Provider
- Required.
- Select the dentist or clinician assigned to this appointment.
- Should be restricted by department or schedule availability.

Date
- Required.
- Must be a valid calendar date.
- Should support date selection from schedule UI.

Time
- Required.
- Should be validated against provider availability and clinic operating hours.

Appointment Type
- Required.
- Select the service or visit category.
- Example values: Consultation, Cleaning, Follow-up, Whitening, Extraction.

Duration
- Optional or required depending on system design.
- Should reflect the estimated procedure time.

Room / Chair
- Optional but recommended.
- Helps staff coordinate room allocation and treatment flow.

Notes
- Optional.
- Stores visit reason, reminders, or special handling instructions.

Status
- Defaults to Scheduled or Pending.
- Can be changed during workflow progression.

Validation
- Required fields must be highlighted immediately if missing.
- Double-booking or time conflict should trigger a warning or block save depending on policy.
- The form should validate the selected date/time against clinic and provider availability.

Save process
- Save creates the appointment record and returns the user to the schedule or detail view.
- Success should show a confirmation message.
- If save fails, the form should retain entered data.

====================================================
9. EDIT APPOINTMENT
====================================================

How editing works
- The Edit Appointment form opens with the current booking details prefilled.
- Users can update permitted fields based on clinic roles and policy.

Editable fields
- Common editable fields include patient, provider, date, time, duration, room, status, appointment type, and notes.
- Appointment ID may be read-only unless a special admin process is allowed.

Audit history
- Changes should be recorded with timestamp, user, and field-level change history where possible.
- This is important for schedule integrity and accountability.

Save
- Updates the existing appointment instead of creating a new one.
- Success should confirm that the schedule item has been updated.

Cancel
- Discards unsaved changes and returns to the previous view.
- If unsaved changes exist, the system should warn the user before leaving.

====================================================
10. APPOINTMENT WORKFLOW
====================================================

Basic patient journey
- A patient request or walk-in is handled by reception.
- A new appointment is created and linked to the patient record.
- The doctor or provider is assigned based on schedule availability.
- The appointment may be confirmed, checked in, completed, or cancelled.

Operational flow
- Reception enters the new appointment.
- The scheduler checks provider availability and room allocation.
- Staff can send reminders through phone, Telegram, or other communication channels.
- On arrival, the appointment is marked as Checked In.
- Once checked in, the patient moves into the Waiting Queue until the dentist is ready to call them to the chair.
- During treatment, staff may add notes and update service status.
- When the dentist calls the patient, the appointment moves to In Chair and is removed from the Waiting Queue.
- After treatment, the appointment is marked as Completed and linked to billing or treatment plan records.

Exceptions
- If the patient is late or missing, the appointment status should change to No Show or Pending Follow-up.
- If a doctor becomes unavailable, rescheduling should be quick and guided by the UI.
- If the appointment needs to be blocked or moved, the user should be able to do so without leaving the current schedule context.

====================================================
11. COMMON UX REQUIREMENTS
====================================================

Consistency
- Appointment-related actions should use the same visual language as the rest of the clinic system.
- Status colors, icons, and buttons should remain recognizable across the app.

Accessibility
- All actions and fields should have labels, visible focus states, and keyboard support.
- Time and status information should be understandable without relying only on color.

Responsiveness
- The schedule view should remain usable on both desktop and tablet layouts.
- Day and week views should support horizontal scrolling or adaptive card layouts when necessary.

Performance
- Schedule loading should be quick even with large appointment datasets.
- Date and filter changes should update smoothly without disrupting input.

Error handling
- Invalid booking time, unavailable provider, or failed save should surface actionable feedback.
- The UI should prevent destructive actions with confirmation messages where necessary.

====================================================
12. SUCCESS METRICS
====================================================

A well-designed Appointments module should support:
- Faster booking of patient visits.
- Reduced double-booking and scheduling conflicts.
- Better visibility of provider workload across the day.
- Clear follow-through from appointment to treatment and billing.
- Simple rescheduling and patient communication workflows.

13. CURRENT IMPLEMENTATION STATUS
====================================================

The Appointments module has been fully implemented with the following components:

- **AppointmentsModule** (`components/appointments/appointments-module.tsx`): Main orchestrator with search, filters, sortable table, pagination, calendar view, waiting queue, check-in and call-patient workflows.

- **Appointment Data** (`components/appointments/appointment-data.ts`): 20+ dummy appointment records with realistic data (patient names, codes, dentists, types, statuses, times, balances, medical alerts, priorities).

- **Appointment Table**: Sortable table with columns — Appointment ID, Patient (with avatar), Patient Code, Dentist, Appointment Type, Date, Time, Status (badge), Check-in (badge), Balance (badge), Actions (View, Edit, Check-in, More menu with Cancel/Reschedule/View Patient/Print).

- **Calendar View** (`AppointmentCalendarPlaceholder`): Month grid with day cells showing appointment counts, color legend (Booked/Checked-in/Completed), week view with appointment cards.

- **Waiting Queue** (`WaitingQueueTable`): Dedicated section below the main table. Only shows Checked-in appointments. Sorted by Emergency first, then appointment time, then check-in time. Columns: Queue No., Patient (with avatar), Patient Code, Dentist, Appointment Time, Check-in Time, Waiting Time (auto-calculated), Priority (Normal/High/Emergency badges), Medical Alert (warning icon), Status, Actions (View, Edit Patient, Call Patient). Call Patient triggers confirmation dialog and navigates to clinical examination.

- **Filters**: Status, Dentist, Appointment Type, Date picker with Today button. Combined filtering with search. Reset Filters action.

- **Search**: Deferred search by patient name, code, phone, or dentist.

- **Pagination**: 6 items per page with page navigation.

- **Summary Cards**: 4 cards at top — Today's Appointments, Checked-in, Waiting, Completed.

- **Check-in Workflow**: Clicking Check-in updates status from Booked/Confirmed to Checked-in, records check-in time, adds to waiting queue.

- **Call Patient Workflow**: Confirmation dialog moves patient from Checked-in to In Chair, removes from waiting queue, navigates to /clinical-examination.

- **Responsive Design**: Full table on desktop, compact table on tablet, card-based layout on mobile for waiting queue.

EXAMINATION / TREATMENT PLANNING MODULE UI SPECIFICATION
Dental Clinic Management System
(Dentist Workspace)

====================================================
1. PAGE OVERVIEW
====================================================

Purpose
The Examination / Treatment Planning page is the clinical workspace for dentists. It is used to perform patient examinations, record clinical findings, chart dental conditions, diagnose problems, and create comprehensive treatment plans.

Who uses it
- Dentists use it as their primary workspace to examine patients, record findings, and plan treatments.
- Dental assistants use it to support the examination process, update charts, and document notes.
- Specialists use it to review referred cases and add specialized treatment recommendations.
- Students or interns use it to learn clinical documentation under supervision.
- Administrators use it to review treatment plans for approval and billing coordination.

Why it exists
- To provide a dedicated digital workspace for clinical examination and treatment planning.
- To replace paper-based dental charts with an interactive digital odontogram.
- To standardize diagnosis recording and treatment plan documentation.
- To connect clinical findings directly to billing, scheduling, and patient records.
- To ensure continuity of care across multiple visits and providers.

How it fits into clinic workflow
- Patients arrive and are checked in through the Appointments module.
- Once checked in, the patient appears in the dentist's examination queue.
- The dentist opens the patient's examination workspace to begin the clinical session.
- Examination findings are recorded, including intraoral and extraoral observations.
- The dentist charts conditions on the digital odontogram (tooth chart).
- Diagnoses are added to the problem list using standardized codes.
- A treatment plan is created with proposed procedures, fees, and priority levels.
- The treatment plan can be presented to the patient for approval.
- Approved treatment plans feed into appointment scheduling and billing.
- Completed examinations are saved as permanent clinical records.

====================================================
2. PAGE LAYOUT
====================================================

Overall structure
The page uses a full-width clinical workspace layout with a fixed application shell, page header, patient selection panel, main examination area, and contextual side panels or modals.

Header
- Located at the top of the page content area.
- Contains the page title, active patient name, and primary clinical actions.
- Keeps key actions visible without forcing users to navigate away from the workspace.

Breadcrumb
- Sits below or near the page title.
- Shows navigation context such as Dashboard > Examination.
- Helps users understand where they are within the clinic system.

Patient selector
- Positioned in the header area or as a top panel.
- Shows the currently selected patient for examination.
- Allows switching between patients in the queue without leaving the workspace.

Search bar
- Positioned in the header area or above the patient queue.
- Supports quick lookup by patient name, patient code, or appointment ID.

Filter bar
- Located next to or below search.
- Provides filtering by examination status, date, treatment plan status, and provider.

Action buttons
- Positioned to the right of the header or in a compact group.
- Includes New Examination, Save, Complete Examination, Print Chart, and Export actions.

Examination workspace
- Main content block occupying the central area.
- Divided into tabbed or sectioned views: Clinical Exam, Dental Chart, Diagnosis, Treatment Plan, Prescription, and Progress Notes.

Patient queue panel
- Left sidebar or collapsible panel showing patients waiting for examination.
- Displays queue position, patient name, appointment time, and priority.

Side panels
- Optional slide-in panel for quick patient history, previous examinations, or reference materials.
- Should not block the whole page unless a destructive action is being confirmed.

Empty state
- Shown when no patient is selected for examination.
- Displays a message such as "Select a patient from the queue to begin examination."
- Encourages users to pick a patient or search for an existing record.

Loading state
- Shown during patient data fetches, chart loading, or save operations.
- Uses skeleton placeholders or shimmer effects.

====================================================
3. HEADER
====================================================

Page title
- Label: Examination / Treatment Planning.
- Indicates the current module and the clinical workspace function.

Active patient indicator
- Shows the name and patient code of the currently selected patient.
- Displays patient age, gender, and any medical alert icons.
- Helps the dentist confirm they are working with the correct patient.

Appointment context
- Shows the current appointment time, type, and provider.
- Links back to the appointment record if needed.

New Examination button
- Primary button.
- Starts a new examination session for the selected patient.
- Used by dentists and assistants to begin clinical documentation.

Save button
- Saves the current examination progress without completing it.
- Allows the dentist to pause and resume later.

Complete Examination button
- Finalizes the examination record.
- Marks the clinical session as complete and triggers follow-up workflows.

View toggle
- Supports switching between Examination, Chart, Treatment Plan, and History views.
- Makes navigation between clinical tasks efficient.

Search
- Quick access search is often placed in the header for immediate patient lookup.
- Accepts patient name, patient code, or appointment ID.

====================================================
4. SEARCH & FILTERS
====================================================

Search by patient name
- Searches patient first name, last name, or full name.
- Supports partial matching and case-insensitive input.
- Results should update as the user types or after pressing Enter.

Patient code
- Finds a patient by their unique patient code or system ID.
- Useful for fast retrieval in busy clinical environments.

Appointment ID
- Finds an examination session by its linked appointment code.
- Useful for troubleshooting and record retrieval.

Dentist / Provider
- Filters examinations by the assigned clinician or provider.
- Important for reviewing personal caseload and team coordination.

Status
- Filters by examination status such as In Progress, Completed, Pending Review, or Cancelled.
- Helps dentists focus on active or incomplete examinations.

Date range
- Filters examinations by day, week, month, or custom date period.
- Supports clinical review and historical record access.

Treatment plan status
- Filters by treatment plan status such as Draft, Proposed, Approved, In Progress, or Completed.
- Useful for tracking treatment plan progression.

Filter behavior
- Filters can be combined.
- Search and filter results should update the patient queue without losing the current examination context.
- Active filters should be visible as chips or tags that can be cleared individually.
- A Reset Filters action should restore the default queue state.

====================================================
5. PATIENT SELECTION & QUEUE
====================================================

Queue behavior
- The patient queue is the primary method for selecting patients for examination.
- Displays patients who have checked in and are waiting for the dentist.
- The queue should update automatically as patients are checked in or moved to treatment.

Queue table columns
- Queue No.
- Patient Name
- Patient Code
- Age / Gender
- Appointment Time
- Check-in Time
- Waiting Time
- Priority
- Medical Alert
- Appointment Type
- Status
- Actions

Queue sorting
- Emergency patients should always appear at the top.
- Followed by High priority, then Normal priority.
- Within the same priority level, sort by Appointment Time (earliest first).
- Within the same time, sort by Check-in Time (earliest first).

Queue actions
- Select Patient: Opens the examination workspace for the selected patient.
- View Appointment: Shows the linked appointment details.
- View Patient Profile: Opens the patient record for quick reference.

Queue empty state
- If no patients are waiting, display a friendly message: "No patients are currently waiting for examination."

Patient history panel
- When a patient is selected, a side panel or section shows:
  - Previous examination records with dates and providers.
  - Past treatment plans and their status.
  - Known medical conditions and allergies.
  - Recent visit notes.

Responsive behavior
- Desktop: full table layout with sidebar queue.
- Tablet: compact table with reduced column width.
- Mobile: card-based layout for queue items.

====================================================
6. EXAMINATION WORKSPACE (MAIN VIEW AREA)
====================================================

Workspace behavior
- The examination workspace is the primary clinical documentation surface.
- Organized into tabbed sections for different clinical tasks.
- Data entered in one section may be referenced in another.

6.1 Clinical Examination Section

Purpose
- Records the dentist's clinical observations during the patient examination.
- Captures both subjective and objective findings.

Subjective findings
- Patient's chief complaint and reason for visit.
- History of present illness or condition.
- Medical history updates and medication changes.
- Pain assessment and symptom description.

Objective findings
- Extraoral examination: facial symmetry, lymph nodes, TMJ assessment.
- Intraoral examination: soft tissue, hard tissue, periodontal assessment.
- Vital signs: blood pressure, heart rate, temperature (if applicable).
- Existing restorations and prosthetics.

Examination fields
- Examination Date (auto-filled with current date).
- Examination Type (New Patient, Periodic, Emergency, Recall).
- Chief Complaint (free text, required).
- History of Present Illness (free text).
- Medical History Review (checkbox list or free text).
- Extraoral Findings (free text with diagram support).
- Intraoral Findings (free text with diagram support).
- Periodontal Assessment (free text or structured form).
- Vital Signs (optional structured fields).
- Clinical Notes (free text for additional observations).

6.2 Dental Chart / Odontogram Section

Purpose
- Provides an interactive visual representation of the patient's dentition.
- Allows the dentist to chart existing conditions, restorations, and treatment needs.

Chart display
- Shows all 32 teeth in standard dental arch layout (upper and lower).
- Supports both universal numbering system and FDI (ISO) notation.
- Each tooth is clickable for detailed examination and charting.

Charting options per tooth
- Present / Missing / Impacted / Unerupted.
- Caries (various surfaces: mesial, distal, buccal, lingual, occlusal).
- Existing restorations (amalgam, composite, crown, bridge, veneer).
- Endodontic status (root canal treated, pulp involvement).
- Periodontal status (mobility, furcation involvement, pocket depth).
- Prosthetic status (implant, denture, partial denture).
- Fracture, attrition, abrasion, erosion.
- Color coding for different conditions.

Chart interaction
- Click a tooth to open a charting dialog or popover.
- Select the condition type and affected surfaces.
- Apply changes that update the visual chart in real time.
- Support for bulk selection (e.g., chart same condition on multiple teeth).

Chart legend
- Displays the color coding and symbols used on the chart.
- Helps new users and assistants interpret the chart quickly.

Chart history
- Shows previous charting records for comparison.
- Allows tracking of changes over time.

6.3 Diagnosis & Problem List Section

Purpose
- Records the dentist's diagnoses based on examination findings.
- Organizes problems in a structured list for treatment planning.

Problem list
- Each problem entry includes:
  - Problem / Diagnosis Name.
  - Diagnosis Code (ICD-10 or dental diagnostic code).
  - Tooth Number (if tooth-specific).
  - Date Identified.
  - Severity (Mild, Moderate, Severe).
  - Status (Active, Resolved, Monitored).
  - Notes.

Adding a diagnosis
- Free text entry with optional code selection from a diagnosis library.
- Auto-suggest common dental diagnoses as the user types.
- Support for multiple diagnoses per examination.

Diagnosis library
- Predefined list of common dental diagnoses with codes.
- Searchable and filterable.
- Customizable by the clinic or organization.

Problem list behavior
- Active problems are displayed prominently.
- Resolved problems are shown with strikethrough or dimmed styling.
- Problems can be linked to treatment plan items.

6.4 Treatment Planning Section

Purpose
- Creates a structured treatment plan based on examination findings and diagnoses.
- Organizes proposed procedures with fees, priority, and sequencing.

Treatment plan overview
- Plan Name or Title (auto-generated or user-defined).
- Plan Date (auto-filled with current date).
- Plan Status (Draft, Proposed, Approved, In Progress, Completed, Cancelled).
- Estimated Total Cost.
- Patient Share and Insurance Share (if applicable).

Treatment plan items
- Each item includes:
  - Procedure Name.
  - Procedure Code (e.g., CDT or local procedure code).
  - Tooth Number (if tooth-specific).
  - Arch / Quadrant.
  - Quantity.
  - Fee / Unit Price.
  - Total Fee.
  - Priority (Urgent, Recommended, Optional).
  - Sequence Order.
  - Status (Planned, Authorized, In Progress, Completed, Cancelled).
  - Notes.

Adding treatment items
- Search and select from a procedure library.
- Auto-populate fee from the clinic's fee schedule.
- Support for multiple items in a single treatment plan.
- Allow reordering of items by drag-and-drop or sequence numbers.

Procedure library
- Predefined list of dental procedures with codes and default fees.
- Searchable and filterable by category.
- Customizable by the clinic.

Treatment plan presentation
- A patient-friendly view of the treatment plan for discussion.
- Shows procedures, fees, and total cost in a clear format.
- Supports printing or sharing via email or Telegram.

Treatment plan approval
- Dentist can mark the plan as Proposed for patient review.
- Patient can approve or request modifications.
- Approved plans are locked from editing and sent to scheduling and billing.

6.5 Prescription Section (Phase 6 — Expanded)

Purpose
- Allows the dentist to create, review, print, and manage prescriptions related to the patient's examination or treatment.
- Prescription is OPTIONAL and should only be created when clinically required.
- Integrates with the patient's recorded allergy and medication history to surface safety alerts BEFORE medication selection.

Layout
- Desktop: Left (70%) Current Prescription / Medication List / Prescription History. Right (30%) Patient Safety Panel + Prescription Summary.
- Tablet: Stack sections vertically.
- Mobile: Single-column layout. Medication tables transform into cards.

Header
- Title: Prescription.
- Subtitle: Create and manage medications prescribed during the patient's care.
- Top-right buttons: + New Prescription, Print Prescription, View History.

Patient Safety Panel (right)
- Highly visible card shown before medication selection.
- Displays: Patient Name, Age, Medical Conditions, Known Allergies, Current Medications, Important Medical Alerts.
- If a known drug allergy exists (e.g., Penicillin), displays a prominent WARNING block (icon + text label, not color-only).
- This is UI-only clinical safety information. No real drug-interaction checking. No automatic medical recommendations.

New Prescription
- Opens a prescription editor (modal/sheet).
- Auto-filled from local UI state: Patient Name (Sofia Martin), Patient Code (PC-1003), Prescription Date (18 Jul 2026), Prescriber (Dr. Maya).
- Fields: Prescription Date, Patient Name, Prescriber, Linked Diagnosis, Linked Treatment, Medication Name, Generic Name, Dosage, Strength, Frequency, Route, Duration, Quantity, Refills, Instructions, Clinical Notes.

Medication Selection
- Searchable medication selector with a dummy drug catalog for dental practice only (Amoxicillin, Azithromycin, Clindamycin, Ibuprofen, Acetaminophen, Chlorhexidine Mouthwash).
- Supports search, select from catalog, and custom medication entry.
- All medication data is dummy UI data for demonstration only — not medical advice.

Allergy Warning UI
- If a selected dummy medication conflicts with a known allergy in the local dummy data (e.g., Amoxicillin vs Penicillin allergy), a warning modal appears: "Medication Safety Warning — This patient has a recorded Penicillin allergy. Selected Medication: Amoxicillin." Buttons: Cancel Selection, Acknowledge Warning.
- No automatic alternative suggestion is made.

Prescription Fields
- Dosage: text input.
- Strength: text input.
- Frequency: dropdown/custom (Once daily, Twice daily, Three times daily, Four times daily, As needed, Custom).
- Route: Oral, Topical, Mouth Rinse, Other.
- Duration: number + unit (e.g., 5 Days, 7 Days).
- Quantity: numeric input.
- Refills: numeric input.
- Instructions: large textarea.
- Clinical Notes: optional textarea.

Multiple Medications
- Multiple medications are permitted in one prescription session.
- Displayed in a Medication List (table on desktop, cards on mobile).
- Columns: Medication, Strength, Dosage, Frequency, Duration, Quantity, Status, Actions (View, Edit, Remove).

Validation
- Required: Medication Name, Dosage, Frequency, Duration, Prescriber.
- Inline validation messages; empty prescriptions cannot be finalized.

Prescription Actions
- Save Draft: keeps prescription editable.
- Finalize Prescription: changes status to Finalized.
- Print Preview: opens print preview.
- Cancel: discards editor.
- After Finalized, editing shows a warning and is treated as an amendment (original is preserved).

Prescription Status
- Supports: Draft, Finalized, Amended, Cancelled.
- Uses status badges with text + icon (not color-only).
- Finalized prescriptions remain in patient history and are never permanently deleted.

Prescription History
- Section listing past prescriptions.
- Columns: Date, Medication, Prescriber, Linked Diagnosis, Status, Actions (View, Print, View Amendments).
- Uses realistic dummy data.

Prescription Detail Panel
- Side panel opened by clicking a prescription.
- Displays: Prescription ID, Date, Patient, Prescriber, Medication List, Dosage Instructions, Linked Diagnosis, Linked Treatment, Clinical Notes, Status, Amendment History.

Print Prescription Preview
- Print-friendly view with: Clinic Logo, Clinic Name, Clinic Contact, Patient Name, Patient Code, Date, Prescriber, Medication Details, Instructions, Prescriber Signature Placeholder, Footer/Disclaimer.
- No actual PDF generation; uses window.print() only.

Empty State
- "No prescriptions recorded for this examination."
- Subtext: "Create a prescription only when clinically required."
- Button: New Prescription.

Loading State
- Skeleton medication rows.

Dummy Data
- Patient: Sofia Martin (PC-1003), Dentist: Dr. Maya.
- Known Allergy: Penicillin. Medical Condition: Diabetes. Current Medication: Metformin.
- Example finalized prescription: Ibuprofen 400 mg, As needed, 5 Days, Prescriber Dr. Maya.

Design Requirements
- Use reusable React components.
- Suggested components: PrescriptionTab, PrescriptionHeader, PatientSafetyPanel, PrescriptionEditor, MedicationSelector, MedicationList, MedicationRow, AllergyWarningModal, PrescriptionHistory, PrescriptionDetailsPanel, PrescriptionPrintPreview, PrescriptionStatusBadge.
- Do not redesign existing Examination components.

Future Compatibility
- Designed to later integrate with: Treatment Execution, Follow-up, Recall, Patient Portal, Telegram Notifications, Billing, Audit Logs, Electronic Prescription Services.
- No backend logic or real medication recommendation logic. All prescription data is dummy UI data only.

6.6 Progress Notes Section (Phase 6 — Expanded)

Purpose
- Provides a chronological clinical record (timeline) of important events, findings, procedures, discussions, and follow-up information.
- Progress Notes should remain available throughout the examination and treatment workflow.

Layout
- Desktop: Main area Clinical Timeline + right side Note Filters and Recent Activity.
- Tablet: Stack sidebar sections below main content.
- Mobile: Single-column. Timeline remains vertical.

Header
- Title: Progress Notes.
- Subtitle: Document chronological clinical notes for this patient's care.
- Top-right button: + Add Note.

Note Types
- Supports: Examination, Procedure, Treatment, Follow-up, Patient Communication, Consultation, Referral, General.
- Uses badges + icons (text + icon, not color-only).

Add Note
- Opens a modal/editor.
- Fields: Note Type, Date and Time (auto-filled), Author (auto-filled Dr. Maya), Linked Examination, Linked Diagnosis, Linked Treatment, Content (rich text), Attachments.
- Rich text support: Bold, Italic, Bullets, Numbered List (no complex document editor).

Attachments
- UI-only attachment selection with chips/cards: Image, X-ray, PDF, Lab Result, Referral Document, Consent Form.
- No backend upload implementation.

Clinical Timeline
- Displays notes newest first by default.
- Each timeline item: Date, Time, Author, Role, Note Type badge, Content Preview, Linked Record, Attachments, Actions (View, Amend, Print).
- Example: "18 Jul 2026 / 10:32 AM / Dr. Maya / Examination — Patient reports pain while chewing on upper left molar. Deep caries observed on tooth 26. Linked: Examination EX-2026-0082".

Note Actions
- View, Amend, Print.
- NO normal Delete for saved clinical notes (preserved in history).

Amend Note
- Opens an amendment modal showing Original Note, Original Author, Original Date.
- Fields: Amendment Reason, Amendment Content. Buttons: Cancel, Save Amendment.
- After saving: original note unchanged; amendment shown below (Amended By, Amendment Date, Reason, Amended Content).

Note Status / Audit
- Every saved note displays Created By, Created At, Last Amended By, Last Amended At (if applicable).
- Supports future clinical audit functionality.

Filters
- Search Notes, Note Type, Author, Date Range, Linked Diagnosis, Linked Treatment, Reset Filters.
- Quick filter chips: All, Examination, Treatment, Follow-up, Communication.

Progress Note Detail Panel
- Side panel: Note Type, Date, Time, Author, Role, Full Content, Linked Examination, Linked Diagnosis, Linked Treatment, Attachments, Amendment History.

Recent Clinical Activity
- Small right-side timeline card showing UI-only activity (e.g., Progress Note Added 10:32 AM, Treatment Plan Updated 10:15 AM, Diagnosis Added 09:58 AM, Odontogram Updated 09:40 AM).

Empty State
- "No progress notes recorded yet."
- Subtext: "Add clinical notes to maintain a chronological record of the patient's care."
- Button: Add First Note.

Loading State
- Skeleton timeline cards.

Design Requirements
- Use reusable React components.
- Suggested components: ProgressNotesTab, ClinicalTimeline, ProgressNoteCard, AddProgressNoteModal, ProgressNoteDetailsPanel, AmendNoteModal, ProgressNoteFilters, RecentClinicalActivity, AttachmentCard.

Clinical Record Rules
- Saved Progress Notes must not be permanently deleted through normal UI actions.
- Corrections to saved notes use amendments; original notes remain visible.
- Every note and amendment shows author and timestamp.
- Progress Notes support links to diagnoses and treatment items (UI-only relationships).

Future Compatibility
- Designed to later integrate with: Treatment Execution, Follow-up, Recall, Patient Portal, Audit Logs, Billing.
- No backend logic. All data is dummy UI data only.

6.7 Diagnosis & Problem List Section

Purpose
- Allows the dentist to record clinical diagnoses after reviewing clinical examination findings, odontogram findings, X-rays, and clinical images.
- The dentist can create multiple diagnoses and associate them with specific teeth when applicable.
- The Diagnosis section will later connect directly to the Treatment Plan.

Layout
- Desktop: Left (70%) Diagnosis & Problem List, Right (30%) Clinical Context Panel.
- Tablet: Diagnosis section on top, Clinical Context below.
- Mobile: Single-column stacked layout.

Header
- Title: Diagnosis & Problem List.
- Subtitle: Record diagnoses based on clinical examination and diagnostic findings.
- Top-right button: + Add Diagnosis.

Diagnosis Summary
- Summary cards at the top displaying: Active Problems, Urgent Problems, Monitored Problems, Resolved Problems.
- Counts update based on local UI state.

Problem List
- Professional table with columns: Diagnosis, Diagnosis Code, Tooth, Severity, Priority, Date Identified, Status, Source, Actions.
- Sortable by Date Identified, Severity, Priority, Tooth Number, Status.
- Default sort order: Emergency first, Urgent second, Recommended third, Routine last.
- Each row supports: Resolve button, More menu (View Details, Add to Treatment Plan, Mark as Resolved).

Diagnosis Status
- Supports: Active, Monitored, Resolved.
- Active uses prominent badge, Monitored uses warning badge, Resolved uses muted/success badge.
- Resolved diagnoses remain visible in patient history.

Severity
- Supports: Mild, Moderate, Severe.
- Uses consistent badges with distinct colors.

Priority
- Supports: Routine, Recommended, Urgent, Emergency.
- Emergency diagnoses are visually prominent with red styling.
- Severity and Priority are treated as separate concepts.

Source
- Shows where the diagnosis originated: Clinical Exam, Odontogram, X-ray, Clinical Image, Manual Entry.
- Uses small badge with icon.

Add Diagnosis Modal
- Clicking "+ Add Diagnosis" opens a modal with fields:
  - Diagnosis Name: Searchable input with auto-suggestion UI (20+ common dental diagnoses).
  - Diagnosis Code: Optional searchable field (ICD-10 placeholder codes).
  - Tooth Selection: Specific Tooth, Multiple Teeth, or General / Full Mouth.
  - Tooth Surface: Optional, supports Occlusal, Mesial, Distal, Buccal, Lingual, Apical. Multiple surfaces can be selected.
  - Severity, Priority, Status, Source: Required dropdowns.
  - Clinical Notes: Large textarea.
- Buttons: Cancel, Save Diagnosis.
- Save adds the diagnosis to the Problem List using local UI state only.

Diagnosis Detail Panel
- Clicking a diagnosis row opens a detail side panel.
- Displays: Diagnosis Name, Code, Tooth/Area, Surfaces, Severity, Priority, Status, Source, Date Identified, Diagnosed By, Clinical Notes, Resolution Date, Linked Evidence.
- Actions: Mark as Resolved, Add to Treatment Plan.

Diagnosis Actions
- Each diagnosis row supports: View, Edit, Mark as Resolved, Add to Treatment Plan, More menu.
- No permanent deletion of clinical diagnoses from normal user actions.

Mark as Resolved
- Opens confirmation modal with: Diagnosis name, Tooth, Resolution Date, Resolution Notes.
- After confirmation: Status changes from Active to Resolved, diagnosis remains visible in history.

Add to Treatment Plan
- UI preparation for Phase 5.
- Opens modal showing: Diagnosis, Tooth, Suggested Procedure, Priority.
- Button: Continue to Treatment Plan (no backend logic yet).

Clinical Context Panel
- Right-side card for quick review of findings while diagnosing.
- Displays: Chief Complaint, Pain Level (visual bar 7/10), Key Clinical Findings, Odontogram Findings, Recent X-ray Findings, Medical Alerts (e.g., Penicillin Allergy).

Linked Evidence
- Inside the Diagnosis Detail panel, a "Linked Evidence" section shows UI references to: Odontogram Tooth, X-ray, Clinical Image, Clinical Examination Finding.
- UI-only links with ExternalLink icons.

Filters
- Above the Problem List: Search Diagnosis, Status Filter, Severity Filter, Priority Filter, Tooth Filter, Source Filter, Reset Filters.
- Quick filter chips: All, Active, Urgent, Emergency, Monitored, Resolved.

Empty State
- Illustration with message: "No diagnoses recorded yet."
- Subtext: "Add a diagnosis based on the patient's clinical examination, odontogram, or diagnostic images."

Loading State

6.8 Treatment Plan & Cost Estimate Section

Purpose
- Allows the dentist to convert diagnosed dental problems into proposed treatments.
- Answers the question: "What treatment does this patient need?"
- Includes: Diagnosis, Proposed procedure, Tooth/teeth, Priority, Treatment sequence, Estimated duration, Estimated cost, Plan status, Patient decision.
- This is a treatment estimate and planning interface. NOT an invoice or payment processor.

Layout
- Desktop: Left (70%) Treatment Plan & Items, Right (30%) Cost Estimate Summary + Details.
- Tablet: Stack sections vertically.
- Mobile: Single-column layout. Treatment items become cards if table is too narrow.

Header
- Title: Treatment Plan.
- Subtitle: Create and manage proposed treatments based on the patient's diagnoses.
- Top-right buttons: + Add Treatment, Save Draft, Present to Patient.

Plan Information
- Summary card displaying: Plan Name (editable in Draft), Plan ID, Plan Date, Created By, Patient, Linked Examination, Plan Status.
- Plan Name is editable while the plan is still in Draft status.

Plan Status
- Supports: Draft, Proposed, Approved, Partially Approved, Declined, In Progress, Completed, Cancelled.
- Uses consistent status badges with distinct colors.
- Draft: Dentist is still creating the plan.
- Proposed: Plan has been presented to the patient.
- Approved: Patient accepted all treatment items.
- Partially Approved: Patient accepted only selected items.
- Declined: Patient declined the proposed plan.
- In Progress: At least one approved treatment has started.
- Completed: All approved treatment items are completed.
- Cancelled: Plan was cancelled.

Treatment Summary Cards
- Display: Total Procedures, Urgent Procedures, Estimated Duration, Estimated Cost, Approved Amount, Remaining Estimate.

Treatment Items Table
- Professional table with columns: Sequence, Procedure, Procedure Code, Linked Diagnosis, Tooth/Area, Priority, Estimated Duration, Unit Price, Quantity, Total, Status, Actions.
- Sortable by Sequence, Procedure, Priority, Price, Total, Status.
- Each row supports: Edit, More menu (Edit Item, Move Up, Move Down, Remove from Plan).

Treatment Item Status
- Supports: Planned, Proposed, Approved, Declined, Scheduled, In Progress, Completed, Cancelled.
- Treatment item status is separate from overall Treatment Plan status.

Add Treatment Modal
- Clicking "+ Add Treatment" opens a modal with fields:
  - Procedure: Searchable input with auto-suggestion (16+ dental procedures).
  - Procedure Code: Optional field with placeholder codes.
  - Linked Diagnosis: Searchable dropdown from Phase 4 diagnoses. Auto-fills tooth when selected.
  - Tooth / Area: Auto-filled from linked diagnosis, manually editable.
  - Tooth Surface: Optional multi-select (Occlusal, Mesial, Distal, Buccal, Lingual, Full).
  - Priority: Emergency, Urgent, Recommended, Optional (defaults from linked diagnosis).
  - Treatment Phase: Emergency Phase, Disease Control Phase, Restorative Phase, Surgical Phase, Prosthetic Phase, Maintenance Phase.
  - Estimated Duration, Quantity, Unit Price, Discount, Notes.
- Buttons: Cancel, Add to Plan.

Priority
- Supports: Emergency, Urgent, Recommended, Optional.
- Default priority may inherit from the linked diagnosis.

Treatment Phases

Purpose

====================================================
11. EXAMINATION WORKFLOW
====================================================

Basic patient journey
- A patient checks in for their appointment.
- The patient appears in the dentist's examination queue.
- The dentist selects the patient and begins the examination.
- Clinical findings are recorded and the dental chart is updated.
- Diagnoses are added to the problem list.
- A treatment plan is created with proposed procedures.
- The examination is completed and saved as a permanent record.

Clinical workflow
- Dentist reviews the patient's medical history and chief complaint.
- Subjective and objective findings are documented.
- The dentist performs intraoral and extraoral examination.
- Findings are charted on the digital odontogram.
- Diagnoses are recorded using standardized codes.
- A treatment plan is developed with prioritized procedures.
- The treatment plan is presented to the patient for discussion.
- If approved, the plan is sent to scheduling and billing.
- Prescriptions are written if needed.
- Progress notes are documented throughout the session.
- The examination is marked as Complete.

Follow-up workflows
- Treatment plan items can be scheduled as future appointments.
- Referrals can be sent to specialists with examination summaries.
- Lab orders can be created for prosthetics or appliances.
- Insurance pre-authorization can be initiated from the treatment plan.

Exceptions
- If the patient needs emergency treatment, the examination can be expedited.
- If the patient cannot complete the examination, it can be saved as In Progress.
- If the diagnosis requires specialist input, a referral can be initiated.
- If the treatment plan is rejected by the patient, it can be revised or archived.

12. COMMON UX REQUIREMENTS
====================================================

Consistency
- Examination-related actions should use the same visual language as the rest of the clinic system.
- Status colors, icons, and buttons should remain recognizable across the app.
- Dental chart symbols and colors should follow standard dental notation conventions.

Accessibility
- All actions and fields should have labels, visible focus states, and keyboard support.
- Clinical information should be understandable without relying only on color.
- The dental chart should support keyboard navigation for users with mobility impairments.

Responsiveness
- The examination workspace should remain usable on both desktop and tablet layouts.
- The dental chart should support touch interaction for tablet use.
- Tabbed sections should collapse into accordion or scrollable sections on smaller screens.

Performance
- Patient data and chart loading should be quick even with large clinical datasets.
- The dental chart should render smoothly with real-time updates.
- Auto-save should not interrupt the clinical workflow.

Error handling
- Invalid data entry, failed saves, or network errors should surface actionable feedback.
- The UI should prevent destructive actions with confirmation messages where necessary.
- Unsaved changes should trigger a warning before navigation away from the workspace.

Data integrity
- Clinical records must be preserved and immutable after completion.
- Amendments should be tracked with version history.
- All changes should be attributed to the logged-in user with timestamps.

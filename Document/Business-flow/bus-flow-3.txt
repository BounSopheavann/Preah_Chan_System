BUSINESS FLOW 3 – EXAMINATION & TREATMENT PLANNING

You are working on a Dental Clinic Management System.

This document is the official explanation of Business Flow 3.

Treat this document as the source of truth.

This document explains how the dentist examines the patient, records
clinical findings, creates a diagnosis, prepares a treatment plan, and
determines whether treatment will begin immediately or be scheduled for
another visit.

Future ERDs, APIs, UI screens, business logic, AI features, and reports
should follow this document.

============================================================ MODULE
PURPOSE ============================================================

Business Flow 3 begins when the patient has already been checked in.

Checked-in ↓ Dentist Calls Patient ↓ Patient Moves to Dental Chair ↓
Clinical Examination ↓ Diagnosis ↓ Treatment Planning ↓ Patient Decision
↓ Proceed to Treatment or Schedule Future Visit

Goal

• Record complete clinical information. • Produce an accurate diagnosis.
• Build a structured treatment plan. • Obtain patient approval. •
Prepare treatment execution.

============================================================ ACTORS
============================================================

Patient

Dentist

Dental Assistant

System

============================================================
PRE-CONDITIONS
============================================================

• Patient has a Checked-in appointment. • Dentist is assigned to the
appointment. • Patient profile exists. • Medical information has already
been verified. • Consent is available or will be confirmed before
treatment.

============================================================ WORKFLOW
============================================================

STEP 1 CALL PATIENT

Business Action

Dentist selects the next patient from today’s waiting queue.

Dental assistant escorts the patient to the treatment room.

System Response

Update appointment status.

Checked-in

↓

In Chair

Optionally record treatment start time.

============================================================

STEP 2 REVIEW PATIENT HISTORY

Business Action

Dentist reviews the patient’s complete history before examination.

Information displayed:

• Previous appointments • Previous treatments • Medical conditions •
Allergies • Current medications • Consent status • X-rays • Clinical
images • Outstanding treatment plans

System Response

Load all related patient records.

Highlight medical alerts.

============================================================

STEP 3 INTERVIEW PATIENT

Business Action

Dentist asks questions including:

• Chief complaint • Symptoms • Pain location • Pain duration • Pain
severity • Previous dental treatment • Current concerns

System Response

Save chief complaint.

Create new clinical note.

============================================================

STEP 4 PERFORM CLINICAL EXAMINATION

Business Action

Dentist performs examination.

Possible findings include:

• Tooth number • Tooth surface • Caries • Fracture • Missing tooth •
Gingival condition • Periodontal condition • Occlusion • Existing
restoration • Mobility • Infection

Additional records

• Clinical photographs • X-rays • Intraoral scans (future)

System Response

Store examination findings.

Attach uploaded files.

============================================================

STEP 5 CREATE DIAGNOSIS

Business Action

Dentist determines diagnosis.

Examples

• Dental caries • Irreversible pulpitis • Periapical abscess •
Gingivitis • Periodontitis • Broken crown • Impacted wisdom tooth

System Response

Create diagnosis record.

Link diagnosis to appointment.

============================================================

STEP 6 CREATE TREATMENT PLAN

Business Action

Dentist creates one or more treatment items.

Each item contains:

• Procedure • Tooth number • Estimated price • Estimated duration •
Priority • Clinical notes

Default Status

Planned

System Response

Save treatment plan.

Create treatment plan items.

============================================================

STEP 7 DISCUSS TREATMENT

Business Action

Dentist explains:

• Diagnosis • Treatment options • Risks • Benefits • Estimated cost •
Estimated visits

Decision

Patient accepts treatment?

YES

→ Continue.

NO

→ Mark treatment items as Declined or Postponed.

System Response

Update treatment plan status.

============================================================

STEP 8 DECIDE WHEN TO TREAT

Decision

Treatment starts today?

YES

→ Continue to Business Flow 4.

NO

→ Receptionist schedules another appointment before patient leaves.

System Response

Save treatment plan.

Keep appointment completed after consultation.

============================================================ VALIDATION
RULES ============================================================

• Only dentists may create diagnoses.

• Only dentists may create treatment plans.

• Every diagnosis belongs to one appointment.

• Every treatment plan belongs to one appointment.

• Every treatment item references one treatment plan.

• Tooth number should be recorded whenever applicable.

• Medical alerts remain visible during examination.

============================================================ BUSINESS
RULES ============================================================

1.  Every examination must produce clinical notes.

2.  Every diagnosis must belong to one appointment.

3.  Treatment planning is performed before treatment unless emergency
    care is required.

4.  Multiple treatment items may exist under one treatment plan.

5.  Patient approval should be obtained before treatment begins.

============================================================ OUTPUTS
============================================================

• Clinical notes

• Diagnosis

• Treatment plan

• Treatment plan items

• Patient decision

============================================================ EXCEPTION
FLOWS ============================================================

Emergency case discovered

→ Skip normal planning if necessary and perform emergency treatment.

Additional X-rays required

→ Suspend diagnosis until imaging is complete.

Patient refuses treatment

→ Record refusal and optionally schedule review.

Medical condition prevents treatment

→ Postpone treatment and record the reason.

============================================================
POST-CONDITIONS
============================================================

• Diagnosis has been recorded.

• Treatment plan exists.

• Patient understands proposed treatment.

• Patient either proceeds to treatment or leaves with a future
appointment.

BUSINESS FLOW 2 – PATIENT ARRIVAL & CHECK-IN

You are working on a Dental Clinic Management System.

This document is the official explanation of Business Flow 2.

Treat this document as the source of truth.

This document explains WHAT happens when a patient arrives at the
clinic, WHO performs each task, HOW the system responds, WHAT
validations occur, and WHICH database records are affected.

Future ERDs, APIs, UI screens, business logic, and automation should
follow this document.

============================================================ MODULE
PURPOSE ============================================================

Business Flow 2 manages the patient’s arrival before clinical treatment
begins.

Booked Appointment ↓ Patient Arrives ↓ Verify Identity ↓ Verify Contact
Information ↓ Verify Medical Information ↓ Check-in ↓ Waiting Queue ↓
Dentist Calls Patient

Goal

• Verify the correct patient. • Keep patient information up to date. •
Ensure medical alerts are reviewed. • Place the patient into today’s
treatment queue. • Prepare the appointment for examination.

============================================================ ACTORS
============================================================

Patient

Receptionist

Dentist

System

============================================================
PRE-CONDITIONS
============================================================

• Patient has a booked appointment. • Receptionist is logged into the
system. • Appointment exists in today’s schedule.

============================================================ WORKFLOW
============================================================

STEP 1 PATIENT ARRIVES

Business Action

Patient arrives at the reception desk.

Receptionist greets the patient.

System Response

No database changes.

============================================================

STEP 2 LOCATE APPOINTMENT

Business Action

Receptionist searches using:

1.  Phone number
2.  Patient code
3.  Patient name
4.  Today’s appointment list

System Response

Search:

patients

appointments

Decision

Appointment found?

YES

→ Open appointment.

NO

→ Continue validation.

Second Decision

Walk-in patient?

YES

→ Create a new same-day appointment if clinic policy allows.

NO

→ Inform patient that no appointment exists.

============================================================

STEP 3 VERIFY PATIENT IDENTITY

Business Action

Receptionist confirms:

• Full name

• Phone number

• Date of birth (optional)

System Response

Display patient profile.

============================================================

STEP 4 VERIFY CONTACT INFORMATION

Business Action

Receptionist asks whether any information has changed.

Examples

• Phone

• Email

• Address

• Emergency contact

Decision

Information changed?

YES

→ Receptionist updates profile.

NO

→ Continue.

System Response

Update patients table.

Save updated_at timestamp.

============================================================

STEP 5 VERIFY MEDICAL INFORMATION

Business Action

Receptionist confirms:

• Allergies

• Medical conditions

• Current medications

• Consent status

Decision

Medical information changed?

YES

→ Update patient profile.

NO

→ Continue.

System Response

Save changes.

Highlight medical alerts for dentist.

============================================================

STEP 6 CHECK-IN PATIENT

Business Action

Receptionist clicks Check-in.

System Response

Update appointment status.

Booked / Confirmed

↓

Checked-in

Record:

• Check-in time (optional)

• User performing check-in

Refresh today’s waiting queue.

============================================================

STEP 7 PATIENT WAITS

Business Action

Patient waits in reception.

System Response

Patient appears in waiting queue.

Queue may be sorted by:

• Appointment time

• Check-in time

• Priority (Emergency)

============================================================

STEP 8 DENTIST CALLS PATIENT

Business Action

Dentist selects next patient.

System Response

Update appointment status.

Checked-in

↓

In Chair

Record treatment start time (optional).

============================================================ VALIDATION
RULES ============================================================

• Appointment must exist before check-in.

• Cancelled appointments cannot be checked in.

• No-show appointments cannot be checked in.

• Only receptionists may perform check-in.

• Patient cannot have multiple active In Chair appointments.

• Consent should be accepted before treatment begins.

• Medical alerts must remain visible throughout treatment.

============================================================ BUSINESS
RULES ============================================================

1.  Verify identity every visit.

2.  Verify contact information every visit.

3.  Verify medical information every visit.

4.  Returning patients are never re-registered.

5.  Receptionist owns check-in.

6.  Dentist changes appointment to In Chair.

============================================================ OUTPUTS
============================================================

• Verified patient profile

• Updated medical information

• Checked-in appointment

• Waiting queue record

============================================================ EXCEPTION
FLOWS ============================================================

Late arrival

→ Receptionist decides whether to continue or reschedule.

Patient arrives too early

→ Patient waits until appointment time.

Patient has no appointment

→ Walk-in workflow if allowed.

Medical alert discovered

→ Notify dentist immediately.

Consent declined

→ Dentist decides whether treatment may continue.

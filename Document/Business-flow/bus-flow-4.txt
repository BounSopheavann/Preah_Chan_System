BUSINESS FLOW 4 – TREATMENT EXECUTION

You are working on a Dental Clinic Management System.

This document is the official explanation of Business Flow 4.

Treat this document as the source of truth.

This document explains how approved treatment plans are carried out, how
completed procedures are recorded, how patient records are updated, and
how treatment is prepared for billing.

Future ERDs, APIs, UI screens, reports, inventory integration, and
billing modules should follow this document.

============================================================ MODULE
PURPOSE ============================================================

Business Flow 4 manages the actual dental treatment.

Approved Treatment Plan ↓ Treatment Begins ↓ Perform Procedures ↓ Record
Clinical Details ↓ Update Treatment Status ↓ Treatment Completed ↓ Ready
for Billing

Goal

• Execute approved treatment safely. • Record every completed procedure.
• Build a complete clinical history. • Prepare completed procedures for
billing.

============================================================ ACTORS
============================================================

Patient

Dentist

Dental Assistant

System

============================================================
PRE-CONDITIONS
============================================================

• Appointment status is In Chair. • Treatment plan exists. • Dentist is
assigned. • Patient has approved treatment (except emergency cases). •
Medical alerts are visible.

============================================================ WORKFLOW
============================================================

STEP 1 REVIEW TREATMENT PLAN

Business Action

Dentist reviews:

• Diagnosis • Planned procedures • Tooth numbers • Previous clinical
notes • X-rays • Medical alerts

System Response

Load treatment plan and patient history.

============================================================

STEP 2 START TREATMENT

Business Action

Dentist begins the first planned procedure.

Dental assistant prepares instruments and materials.

System Response

Update treatment item status.

Planned

↓

In Progress

============================================================

STEP 3 PERFORM PROCEDURE

Business Action

Dentist performs treatment.

Examples

• Scaling • Filling • Extraction • Root Canal • Crown Preparation •
Crown Delivery • Denture Adjustment • Emergency Treatment

Assistant provides chairside support.

============================================================

STEP 4 RECORD CLINICAL DETAILS

Business Action

Dentist records:

• Procedure performed • Tooth number • Tooth surface • Materials used •
Local anesthesia • Clinical notes • Prescription • Complications (if
any)

Assistant may upload:

• Clinical photos • X-rays • Supporting documents

System Response

Create procedure record.

Store attachments.

============================================================

STEP 5 UPDATE TREATMENT STATUS

Decision

Procedure completed?

YES

→ Mark procedure Completed.

NO

→ Keep status In Progress or Planned.

Decision

More procedures remaining today?

YES

→ Continue next treatment item.

NO

→ Continue to final review.

System Response

Update treatment plan items.

============================================================

STEP 6 FINAL CLINICAL REVIEW

Business Action

Dentist reviews completed treatment.

Decision

Additional visit required?

YES

→ Create remaining planned treatment items.

→ Recommend next appointment.

NO

→ Finish today’s treatment.

System Response

Save treatment summary.

============================================================

STEP 7 COMPLETE APPOINTMENT

Business Action

Dentist confirms treatment is finished.

System Response

Update appointment status.

In Chair

↓

Completed

Generate billable procedure list.

Mark completed procedures as ready for invoice generation.

============================================================ VALIDATION
RULES ============================================================

• Only dentists may complete procedures.

• Procedures cannot be completed before treatment starts.

• Every completed procedure belongs to one appointment.

• Tooth number should be recorded whenever applicable.

• Completed procedures cannot be deleted directly.

• Changes after completion should be tracked by audit logs.

============================================================ BUSINESS
RULES ============================================================

1.  Only approved procedures should be performed.

2.  Clinical notes should be recorded immediately after treatment.

3.  Multiple procedures may be completed during one appointment.

4.  Incomplete procedures remain available for future appointments.

5.  Every completed procedure becomes eligible for billing.

============================================================ OUTPUTS
============================================================

• Completed procedure records

• Updated clinical history

• Updated treatment plan

• Appointment marked Completed

• Billable procedures

============================================================ EXCEPTION
FLOWS ============================================================

Patient requests to stop treatment

→ Record completed work.

→ Leave remaining procedures Planned.

→ Schedule another visit.

Medical emergency occurs

→ Stop treatment immediately.

→ Record incident.

→ Provide emergency care.

Equipment failure

→ Pause treatment.

→ Reschedule remaining procedures.

Additional treatment discovered

→ Update treatment plan before continuing.

============================================================
POST-CONDITIONS
============================================================

• Treatment history is complete.

• Clinical notes are saved.

• Completed procedures are ready for invoice generation.

• Appointment is completed.

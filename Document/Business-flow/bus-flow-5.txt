BUSINESS FLOW 5 – BILLING & PAYMENT

You are working on a Dental Clinic Management System.

This document is the official explanation of Business Flow 5.

Treat this document as the source of truth.

This document explains how completed dental procedures are converted
into invoice items, how payments are processed, how outstanding balances
are managed, and how the patient’s financial records are updated.

Future ERDs, APIs, accounting modules, dashboards, reports, and payment
integrations should follow this document.

============================================================ MODULE
PURPOSE ============================================================

Business Flow 5 manages the clinic’s financial workflow after treatment
has been completed.

Completed Appointment ↓ Generate Invoice ↓ Review Charges ↓ Receive
Payment ↓ Generate Receipt ↓ Update Patient Balance ↓ Ready for
Follow-up / Recall

Goal

• Automatically generate invoices from completed procedures. • Record
accurate payments. • Track outstanding balances. • Produce receipts. •
Maintain complete financial history.

============================================================ ACTORS
============================================================

Patient

Receptionist

Administrator

System

============================================================
PRE-CONDITIONS
============================================================

• Appointment status is Completed. • One or more completed procedures
exist. • Billable procedures are available.

============================================================ WORKFLOW
============================================================

STEP 1 GENERATE INVOICE

Business Action

Receptionist opens the completed appointment.

System Response

Automatically retrieve:

• Completed procedures • Treatment prices • Quantity • Existing
discounts (if applicable)

Generate invoice draft.

============================================================

STEP 2 REVIEW INVOICE

Business Action

Receptionist reviews:

• Procedures • Unit prices • Quantity • Discounts • Taxes (if
applicable) • Grand total

Decision

Invoice correct?

YES

→ Continue.

NO

→ Administrator reviews pricing if price adjustment is required.

System Response

Recalculate totals automatically.

============================================================

STEP 3 PATIENT MAKES PAYMENT

Business Action

Receptionist asks for payment.

Supported methods

• Cash • Credit Card • Bank Transfer • QR Payment • Other electronic
payment

Decision

Full payment?

YES

→ Continue.

NO

→ Record partial payment.

System Response

Calculate:

• Amount paid • Remaining balance

============================================================

STEP 4 RECORD PAYMENT

Business Action

Receptionist records:

• Payment amount • Payment method • Payment reference • Notes (optional)

System Response

Create payment record.

Update invoice balance.

============================================================

STEP 5 GENERATE RECEIPT

Business Action

Receptionist provides receipt.

Receipt may be:

• Printed • PDF • Telegram (future) • Email (future)

System Response

Generate receipt number.

Store receipt history.

============================================================

STEP 6 UPDATE FINANCIAL STATUS

Decision

Balance remaining?

NO

→ Invoice status

Paid

YES

→ Invoice status

Partially Paid

System Response

Update patient outstanding balance.

============================================================

STEP 7 FINISH VISIT

Business Action

Receptionist confirms payment is complete.

Patient leaves clinic.

System Response

Close billing workflow.

Patient becomes eligible for Business Flow 6.

============================================================ VALIDATION
RULES ============================================================

• Only completed procedures may appear on invoices.

• Every payment belongs to one invoice.

• Invoice total cannot be negative.

• Partial payments are allowed.

• Payment amount cannot exceed outstanding balance unless overpayment is
supported.

• Administrators control pricing changes.

============================================================ BUSINESS
RULES ============================================================

1.  Completed procedures automatically become invoice items.

2.  Receptionists collect payments.

3.  Administrators manage service pricing.

4.  Every payment must have an audit history.

5.  Outstanding balances remain visible on the patient profile.

============================================================ OUTPUTS
============================================================

• Invoice

• Invoice items

• Payment record

• Receipt

• Updated patient balance

============================================================ EXCEPTION
FLOWS ============================================================

Patient cannot pay today

→ Leave invoice unpaid or partially paid.

→ Record outstanding balance.

Incorrect invoice detected

→ Correct invoice before payment.

Payment transaction fails

→ Retry payment or select another payment method.

Refund requested

→ Administrator follows refund policy and records adjustment.
